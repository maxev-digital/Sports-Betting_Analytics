## 🎓 What is Arbitrage Betting?

Arbitrage betting (or "arbing") is a strategy that **guarantees profit** by exploiting price differences between sportsbooks. You place bets on all possible outcomes of an event across different bookmakers, ensuring you profit regardless of the result.

### The Core Concept

Every betting odd has an "implied probability" - the bookmaker's assessment of the likelihood of that outcome:

**Conversion Formula:**
```
Positive odds (+150): Implied Prob = 100 / (odds + 100) = 100 / 250 = 40%
Negative odds (-110): Implied Prob = 110 / (110 + 100) = 52.4%
```

**Normal Situation (No Arbitrage):**
- Team A at -110 → 52.4% implied probability
- Team B at -110 → 52.4% implied probability
- **Total: 104.8%** ← Bookmaker has 4.8% edge (the "vig")

**Arbitrage Opportunity:**
- Team A at +110 (Book 1) → 47.6% implied probability
- Team B at +114 (Book 2) → 46.7% implied probability
- **Total: 94.3%** ← YOU have a 5.7% guaranteed profit!

## 🎯 Why Do These Opportunities Exist?

### 1. **Independent Pricing Models**

Each sportsbook uses proprietary algorithms:
- **BetMGM** might price Utah Mammoth differently than **FanDuel**
- Different customer bases create different risk exposures
- Books don't coordinate pricing (that would be illegal)

### 2. **Market Inefficiencies**

Books update at different speeds:
- Sharp books (Pinnacle, Circa) move lines instantly
- Recreational books (FanDuel, DraftKings) may lag 30-60 seconds
- Regional books wait for consensus

When injury news breaks or a sharp bet lands, arbitrage windows open.

### 3. **Promotional Odds**

Books boost odds to attract customers:
- "Odds Boost" promotions create intentional arbitrage
- New market states have aggressive pricing to gain market share
- **Fanatics** and **William Hill** often offer competitive lines

### 4. **Lower Volume Markets**

**NHL has 14/20 opportunities (70%)** because:
- Lower betting volume → less efficient pricing
- Fewer sharp bettors → slower corrections
- More variance → books price conservatively

### 5. **Computational Lag**

Our system scans every 10 seconds. Books update every 30-60 seconds. This creates brief windows where mispricing exists across the market.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake #1: Ignoring Betting Limits

You calculate a perfect $1,000 arbitrage, but:
- Book A limits you to $250
- Book B limits you to $400
- Your stakes don't align

**Solution:**
- Know limits before betting
- Start small ($100-200 per arb)
- Build account history with recreational bets

### ❌ Mistake #2: Wrong Line/Total

Multiple lines exist for the same game:
```
Lakers -4.5 @ -110
Lakers -5.0 @ +100
Lakers -5.5 @ +120
```

Betting Lakers -4.5 at one book and Warriors +5.5 at another **looks like** arbitrage but has a 1-point gap.

**Solution:**
- Verify exact lines match
- Or ensure the gap creates a profitable "middle"

### ❌ Mistake #3: Different Markets

Make sure both books offer the same thing:
- Full game vs. 1st half
- Regulation vs. including overtime
- Listed pitcher (MLB) vs. action

**Solution:**
- Read market rules carefully
- Our system filters for matching markets automatically

### ❌ Mistake #4: Accounts Get Limited

After consistent arbitrage profits, books will:
- Limit your bet sizes
- Require manual review of bets
- Ban you entirely

**Solution:**
- Mix in recreational bets at bad odds
- Bet popular markets occasionally (NFL primetime)
- Don't max out every arbitrage
- Spread action across many books
