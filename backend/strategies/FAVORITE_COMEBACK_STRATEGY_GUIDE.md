## <a name="regression"></a>Understanding Regression to the Mean

### What is Regression to the Mean?

Regression to the mean is a statistical phenomenon where extreme measurements tend to be followed by measurements closer to the average.

**Basketball Examples:**

1. **Shooting Percentages**
   - Team shoots 65% in Q1 (season avg: 46%)
   - Over the next 36 minutes, expect reversion toward 46%
   - The further from average, the stronger the pull back

2. **Scoring Pace**
   - Team scores 35 points in Q1 (season avg: 28 PPQ)
   - Unsustainable hot streak, likely to cool off
   - Favorite scoring 25 in Q1 (season avg: 30 PPQ) likely to heat up

### Why It Happens in Basketball

1. **Small Sample Size**
   - Q1 = only 12 minutes of play
   - A few made/missed shots swing percentages wildly
   - Season averages based on 1,000+ shot attempts

2. **Defense Adjustments**
   - Coaches make halftime adjustments
   - Identify and close hot shooting lanes
   - Change defensive schemes to disrupt rhythm

3. **Player Fatigue**
   - Hot shooting requires energy and focus
   - Underdogs playing above their heads tire faster
   - Favorites conserve energy early, turn it on late

4. **Talent Gap**
   - Season statistics reflect true talent level
   - Short-term variance doesn't change long-term ability
   - Better teams have deeper benches, more weapons

**Expected 2H Lakers Scoring (3 quarters):**
```
True talent: 115 PPG / 4 quarters = 28.75 PPQ
Regression factor: +4 points (below-average Q1)
Expected 2H: (28.75 * 3) + 4 = 90.25 points
```

**Expected 2H Pistons Scoring:**
```
True talent: 105 PPG / 4 quarters = 26.25 PPQ
Regression factor: -6 points (above-average Q1)
Expected 2H: (26.25 * 3) - 6 = 72.75 points
```

**Predicted 2H Outcome:**
```
Lakers 90.25 - Pistons 72.75 = Lakers by 17.5
Against spread: 17.5 - 5.5 = Lakers cover by 12 points
```

**This is a STRONG BET** ✓

Net Profit: $1,600

## <a name="risk-management"></a>Risk Management

### Bankroll Management (Kelly Criterion)

The Kelly Criterion helps determine optimal bet sizing:

**Formula:**
```
Kelly % = (Win% × Odds) - (Loss% / Odds)
```

**For 60% win rate at -110 odds:**
```
Kelly % = (0.60 × 1.91) - (0.40 / 1.91)
        = 1.146 - 0.209
        = 0.937 or ~9.4% of bankroll
```

**Fractional Kelly (Recommended):**
- **Full Kelly = 9.4%** (aggressive, high variance)
- **Half Kelly = 4.7%** (moderate)
- **Quarter Kelly = 2.35%** (conservative, recommended)

**MAX-EV-SPORTS Recommendation:**
- HIGH confidence: 3-4% bankroll
- MEDIUM confidence: 2-3% bankroll
- LOW confidence: 1-2% bankroll

### Position Sizing by Confidence

| 6-9 | LOW | 53% | 2.9% | 1-2% (½ Kelly) |

### Stop-Loss Rules

## <a name="mistakes"></a>Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Betting Every Favorite That Trails

**Mistake:** "The Warriors are down after Q1, auto-bet 2H!"

**Problem:** Not all trailing favorites have regression indicators

**Solution:** Use the scoring system. If score <6, PASS.

---

### 2. Ignoring Talent Gap

**Mistake:** Betting a 2-point favorite trailing by 8

**Problem:** Small talent gap + large deficit = legitimate underdog threat

**Solution:** Require minimum 5 PPG talent gap for bets

---

### 3. Overvaluing Recent Performance

**Mistake:** "The Pistons beat the Heat last week, they're legit!"

**Problem:** Small sample recency bias

**Solution:** Trust season-long statistics (500+ possessions)

---

### 4. Poor Line Shopping

**Mistake:** Betting Celtics -7 (2H) at first book you check

**Problem:** Leaving money on the table

**Solution:** Check 3-5 books, always get best price (Celtics -6.5 is 50% better than -7.5!)

---

### 5. Betting Too Large

**Mistake:** "This is a lock! Bet 20% of bankroll!"

**Problem:** Even 65% winners lose 35% of the time

**Solution:** Never exceed 4% per bet, regardless of confidence

---

### 6. Ignoring Situational Factors

**Mistake:** Not checking injury reports, rest days, travel

**Problem:** Missing key information that affects regression

**Solution:** Always verify:
- Key player injuries
- Back-to-back games
- Rest differential
- Travel schedule (coast-to-coast, etc.)

---

### 7. Tilting After Losses

**Mistake:** Losing 2 bets, then betting 3H spread to "get it back"

**Problem:** Emotional betting destroys bankroll

**Solution:** Stick to system. If down 2 units, take break.
